How Our Prediction Model Works

Prediction Methodology

Our house price prediction system uses a linear regression model that considers multiple factors that influence property prices in the Indian real estate market. The model is based on the following equation:

Price = Base Price + (Location Factor × City Coefficient) + (Area × Area Rate) + (Bedrooms × Bedroom Value) + ...

Each parameter is weighted according to its influence on property prices based on historical data and current market trends.

Key Features of Our Model
  • Location-Specific Pricing: Our model adjusts for city-specific price variations. Metropolitan areas like Mumbai and Delhi have higher coefficients compared to tier-2 and tier-3 cities.
  • Area Calculation: The price per square foot varies across cities but is a significant factor in all locations.
  • Configuration Impact: The number of bedrooms and bathrooms directly influences property value.
  • Age Depreciation: The model accounts for property age, with newer properties having higher values.
  • Floor Premium: Higher floors typically command higher prices, especially in high-rise buildings.
  • Furnishing Status: Fully furnished properties have a premium over unfurnished ones.
  • Amenity Value: Each amenity adds incremental value to the property.
Model Limitations

While our model provides a reasonable estimate, it has some limitations:

  • It doesn't account for micro-market variations within cities
  • Property view, orientation, and floor plan are not considered
  • Market fluctuations and seasonal trends may affect actual prices
  • Unique property features or legal issues are not factored in
Did You Know?

According to recent studies, location accounts for approximately 40-50% of a property's value in most Indian cities, followed by size (20-30%) and amenities (10-15%).

Indian Real Estate Market Overview

Current Market Trends

The Indian real estate market has been evolving rapidly over the past decade. Key trends include:

Urban Growth

Rapid urbanization is driving demand for housing in metropolitan areas and satellite towns.

Affordable Housing

Government initiatives are promoting affordable housing projects across the country.

Luxury Segment

Premium housing with world-class amenities is growing in major cities.

Sustainable Housing

Eco-friendly and energy-efficient housing projects are gaining popularity.

Regional Variations

India's real estate market shows significant regional differences:

  • Mumbai: Highest property prices in India, with significant premium for sea-facing properties
  • Delhi-NCR: Wide price variation between central Delhi and surrounding regions like Gurgaon and Noida
  • Bangalore: IT industry drives property demand, with higher prices in areas near tech parks
  • Hyderabad: Experiencing rapid growth with relatively more affordable prices than other metros
  • Chennai: Stable market with consistent growth, especially in southern and western zones
  • Pune: Popular among young professionals with reasonable prices compared to Mumbai
